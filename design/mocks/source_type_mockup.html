How to Cite a Journal Article in APA 7th Edition

A comprehensive guide to citing journal articles in APA format, with examples for every variation you'll encounter.

Basic Format Explanation

Journal articles are one of the most commonly cited sources in academic writing. The basic format includes the author(s), publication year, article title, journal name, volume and issue numbers, page range, and DOI or URL.

The key to proper journal article citations is understanding which elements to italicize (journal name and volume number) and which to include in parentheses (issue number and publication year).

Key Elements to Remember

  • Author names: Last name, First initial. Middle initial.
  • Article title: Use sentence case (only first word capitalized)
  • Journal name: Use title case and italicize
  • Volume: Italicize the volume number
  • Issue: Include in parentheses (not italicized)
  • DOI: Format as https://doi.org/... (no period at end)

Required vs Optional Elements

Required Elements

Author(s)

At least one author is required. List up to 20 authors before using "et al."

Publication Year

The year the article was published. Place in parentheses after authors.

Article Title

The title of the article in sentence case. Include subtitle after colon.

Journal Name

The full name of the journal in title case, italicized.

Volume Number

The volume number, italicized. Do not include "Vol."

Optional Elements

Issue Number

Include when available. Place in parentheses after volume (not italicized).

Page Range

Include if article has page numbers. Use format: 123-145.

DOI or URL

Include DOI when available (preferred). Otherwise include URL.

üìå Missing Elements Rule:

If an element is missing, simply omit it and move to the next element. Don't use "n.d." or placeholders unless specifically indicated (like for missing dates).

In-Text Citation Guidelines

When citing journal articles in the text of your paper, follow these guidelines:

Parenthetical Citation

Recent research demonstrates a strong correlation between sleep and academic performance (Smith & Jones, 2020).

Narrative Citation

Smith and Jones (2020) found that students who maintained regular sleep schedules performed better academically.

Multiple Authors (3+)

First citation: (Anderson, Shibuya, Ihori, et al., 2010)
Subsequent citations: (Anderson et al., 2010)
Note:

In APA 7, use "et al." for 3 or more authors in all in-text citations (both first and subsequent). This is different from APA 6, which required listing all authors in the first citation.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these steps to create a perfect journal article citation:

  1. Identify all authors: Find all author names on the article. Format each as Last name, First initial. Middle initial. Separate with commas and use "&" before the last author.
  2. Locate the publication year: Find the year on the article or journal issue. Place in parentheses after authors.
  3. Copy the article title: Type the exact article title. Convert to sentence case (only first word and proper nouns capitalized).
  4. Find the journal name: Locate the journal name. Keep in title case and italicize. Do not italicize the comma after it.
  5. Get volume and issue: Find these on the article or journal cover. Italicize volume, place issue in parentheses (not italicized).
  6. Note the page range: Find the first and last page numbers. Format as start-end (e.g., 123-145).
  7. Include the DOI: If available, format as https://doi.org/... No period at the end. If no DOI, use the journal's homepage URL.
  8. Verify formatting: Check punctuation, italics, and capitalization. Ensure no elements are missing.

Validation Checklist

Before submitting, verify your journal article citations with this checklist:

  • All authors listed with correct format (Last, F. M.)
  • Ampersand (&) used before last author
  • Publication year in parentheses
  • Article title in sentence case
  • Journal name in title case and italicized
  • Volume number italicized
  • Issue number in parentheses (not italicized)
  • Page range included (if available)
  • DOI formatted as https://doi.org/...
  • No period after DOI or URL
  • All punctuation correct (commas, periods)
