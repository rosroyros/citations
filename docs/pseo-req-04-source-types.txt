```markdown
## Basic Format

### Reference List Format

The general format for a journal article in APA 7th edition is:

```
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Title of Journal, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
```

**Element Breakdown:**

1. **Author(s):** List all authors up to 20
   - Format: Last name, First initial., Middle initial.
   - Punctuation: Comma after each author, ampersand (&) before last author, period after author group
   - Example: Smith, J. K., & Jones, M. L.

2. **Date:** Year of publication in parentheses
   - Format: (Year)
   - Punctuation: Period after closing parenthesis
   - Example: (2020).

3. **Article title:** Use sentence case
   - Format: Capitalize only first word, first word after colon, and proper nouns
   - Punctuation: Period at end
   - Italics: NO - do not italicize article titles
   - Example: The impact of social media on adolescent development.

4. **Journal title:** Use title case
   - Format: Capitalize all major words
   - Punctuation: Comma after
   - Italics: YES - italicize entire journal name
   - Example: *Journal of Adolescent Psychology*,

5. **Volume:** Number only
   - Format: Just the number, no "vol." or "v." prefix
   - Punctuation: No punctuation after volume
   - Italics: YES - italicize volume number
   - Example: *15*

6. **Issue:** Number in parentheses
   - Format: (Number)
   - Punctuation: Comma after closing parenthesis
   - Italics: NO - do not italicize issue number
   - Example: (2),
   - Note: Always required in APA 7 (change from APA 6)

7. **Pages:** Page range with en dash
   - Format: Start–End (no spaces, use en dash not hyphen)
   - Punctuation: Period after page numbers
   - Prefix: Do NOT use "pp." for journal articles
   - Example: 145–167.

8. **DOI:** Formatted as URL
   - Format: https://doi.org/xxxxx
   - Punctuation: No period after DOI
   - When to include: Always if available (preferred over URL)
   - Example: https://doi.org/10.1037/amp0000151

### In-Text Citation Format

When citing a journal article in the text of your paper:

**Parenthetical citation:**
- Format: (Author, Year)
- Example: (Smith, 2020)
- Use: At end of sentence containing cited information

**Narrative citation:**
- Format: Author (Year)
- Example: Smith (2020) found that...
- Use: When author name is part of your sentence

**With page numbers** (for direct quotes):
- Format: (Author, Year, p. X)
- Example: (Smith, 2020, p. 156)
```

#### **IN-TEXT CITATION GUIDELINES**

```markdown
## In-Text Citation Guidelines

### Basic Rules

**One author:**
- Parenthetical: ([Last name], [Year])
- Narrative: [Last name] ([Year])
- Example: (Smith, 2020) or Smith (2020)

**Two authors:**
- Parenthetical: ([Author 1] & [Author 2], [Year])
- Narrative: [Author 1] and [Author 2] ([Year])
- Example: (Smith & Jones, 2020) or Smith and Jones (2020)
- Note: Use "&" in parenthetical, "and" in narrative

**Three or more authors:**
- Parenthetical: ([First Author] et al., [Year])
- Narrative: [First Author] et al. ([Year])
- Example: (Smith et al., 2020) or Smith et al. (2020)
- Note: Use "et al." from first citation (changed in APA 7)

### Special Situations

**No author:**
- Use title in place of author
- Parenthetical: (["Title"], [Year])
- Example: ("Study Shows," 2020)
- Note: Use quotation marks if title is not italicized in reference

**No date:**
- Use "n.d." instead of year
- Example: (Smith, n.d.)

**Direct quotes:**
- Always include page number
- Example: (Smith, 2020, p. 45)
- Multiple pages: (Smith, 2020, pp. 45-47)

**Paraphrasing:**
- Page number optional but recommended
- Example: (Smith, 2020) or (Smith, 2020, p. 45)

### Multiple Citations

**Same author, different years:**
- List chronologically
- Example: (Smith, 2018, 2020, 2022)

**Same author, same year:**
- Add lowercase letters
- Example: (Smith, 2020a, 2020b)
- Letters must match those in reference list

**Different authors in one citation:**
- List alphabetically, separate with semicolons
- Example: (Jones, 2019; Smith, 2020; Williams, 2021)
```
