**Example for Capitalization:**
```markdown
## What Correct Capitalization Looks Like

### The Rule
APA uses different capitalization rules for different elements:
- **Article/chapter titles:** Sentence case (capitalize only first word, first word after colon, proper nouns)
- **Journal/book titles:** Title case (capitalize all major words)
- **Proper nouns:** Always capitalized regardless of position

**Official APA Guidance:**
> "In general, capitalize only the first word of a title, subtitle, or heading, and any proper nouns. Capitalize all major words in journal titles" (APA, 2020, p. 165-166).

### Visual Examples

**Correct Article Title:**
‚úÖ The impact of social media on adolescent development

**Incorrect Article Title:**
‚ùå The Impact of Social Media on Adolescent Development

**Correct Journal Title:**
‚úÖ *Journal of Adolescent Psychology*

**Incorrect Journal Title:**
‚ùå *Journal of adolescent psychology*

### Key Rules to Remember

1. **Article/chapter titles use sentence case:**
   - Capitalize first word only
   - Capitalize first word after colon or dash
   - Keep proper nouns capitalized
   - Example: "Therapy outcomes in New York City hospitals"

2. **Journal/book titles use title case:**
   - Capitalize all major words (4+ letters)
   - Capitalize all nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs
   - Example: *Journal of Experimental Social Psychology*

3. **Proper nouns always capitalized:**
   - Place names, brand names, acronyms
   - Example: "American Psychological Association"

### Special Considerations
- Hyphenated words: Capitalize both parts (Natural-Born)
- After colons: Always capitalize first word after colon
- Database names: Capitalize as proper nouns (PubMed, Google Scholar)
```
